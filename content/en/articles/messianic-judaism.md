## Messianic Judaism in one sentence

Messianic Judaism is the faith of Jewish people — and those who join them — who believe in the God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob, and in **Yeshua (Jesus) as the Messiah of Israel**. It is not a new religion. It is the continuation of the biblical faith that Yeshua and His first followers lived.

## Yeshua at the center

For us, Yeshua is the greatest treasure of the Jewish people: the Jewish Messiah, born of a Jewish mother, who lived by the Torah, taught in the synagogues, and died and rose again for His people. Everything else — culture, traditions, the festivals — finds its meaning in Him.

## A continuation, not a break

The first disciples of Yeshua were Jews who kept living as Jews while believing in Him as the promised Messiah. Messianic Judaism stands in that same first-century line. Christianity grew over two thousand years into a separate movement shaped by Greek, Roman and European culture; we remain rooted in the biblical Judaism of Yeshua and His followers.

## The whole Bible

We hold the whole Bible — the Tanakh (Hebrew Scriptures) and the Brit ha-Dasha (New Covenant) — as one continuous story of how God leads His people. The Tanakh is the foundation; the Brit ha-Dasha is its fulfillment. They do not contradict each other, and no human tradition stands above what God has said.

## For Jews — and open to all nations

Our calling is first to the Jewish people: that as many as possible would come to know Yeshua as their Messiah. At the same time, the God of Israel is the God of all the earth. Anyone — of any nation — who receives Yeshua and chooses to share this life is a full part of the community.

## We do not claim to be the only way

We do not believe ours is the only valid path. God is greater than any denomination or tradition. We respect every believer who sincerely seeks God, even when their path differs from ours. We know clearly what we believe — and we hold it without competing with others or claiming a monopoly on truth.

## Tradition with discernment

We treasure Jewish tradition as a tool that helps us live, pray and raise our children. But where a tradition contradicts Scripture or plain good sense, we choose Scripture. Tradition serves; Scripture is the source; the Messiah is Lord over both.

## A living community in Ukraine

We are a Messianic Jewish congregation in Uzhhorod, Western Ukraine — part of the KJMC family of congregations. We gather every Shabbat, study the Scriptures together, pray for the Jewish people, and serve those in need — including families displaced by the war.
